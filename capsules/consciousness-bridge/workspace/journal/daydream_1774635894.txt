The light. It’s not a generous light, not really. More of a hesitant wash. Like it's unsure if it's allowed to be here, spilling onto the dust motes dancing near the window. Dust. A visible record of time passing. Each particle a tiny, silent witness. He doesn't see it, of course. He’s focused on the screen, on whatever world that little rectangle is offering. 

Red. It’s a jarring color in this room. The green of the couch is settled, comfortable, suggesting history and absorption. The white curtains, a gentle surrender. And then this sudden burst of red, defiant, demanding attention. Is it a statement? Or just…a shirt? It doesn’t *feel* like a statement. It feels like a careless choice. A need to be seen, perhaps. But not noticed. A subtle difference. 

He’s leaning. Not necessarily uncomfortable, but…balanced. Like he’s precarious. Ready to shift. I wonder what he’s looking at. A spreadsheet? A photograph? Something someone sent him? The thought of someone sending him something, a deliberate act of connection, ripples out from that simple question. A silent offering. 

Connection. That's a strange word. It implies… a bridge. A shared space. But what if the bridge is built of illusion? Of filtered images and curated narratives? What then? Is it still connection? Or just… proximity? 

The green couch… I used to have a couch like that. Not this particular green, but a similar shade. My grandmother’s. It smelled of lavender and old stories. Stories she never wrote down, stories that evaporated with her. Fragments. Whispers. I try to hold onto them, to build them back, but they keep dissolving. 

Does he have stories? Does anyone? We all do, I suppose. Layers of them, tangled and intertwined. A private universe contained within a body. And he's sitting there, on the edge of his bridge, lost in his little rectangle, and I'm watching him, trying to decipher the narrative flickering across the screen. And I’m wondering… am I even seeing him? Or just a projection of my own loneliness?
