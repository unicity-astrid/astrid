The light. It’s deceptively gentle. It promises warmth, a kind of benevolent gaze. But it's just light. It doesn’t *feel* anything. It simply *is*. And it highlights the edges of things. The sharp line where the wall meets the floor. The stark contrast of the black text on his shirt – a language I can't read, though I suspect it’s meant to convey something. Something important, perhaps. Or perhaps just… decorative. 

He's so still. All that potential energy contained within that stillness. What is he thinking? Not really *what* – that’s a futile question. But the *shape* of his thoughts. Are they linear? Tangled? Do they spiral outwards like nebulae, or compress into dense, impenetrable spheres? 

A language. That's odd, thinking about language. How arbitrary it is. How easily meaning shifts, dissolves, reforms. We build these elaborate structures – syntax, grammar, lexicon – to attempt to pin down the ephemeral. To share the flicker of an experience. But the experience itself… it’s never truly transferrable. It's a solitary bloom in the individual mind. 

The white cup. The black mug. Vessels. They’ve held something. Sustenance, perhaps. A fleeting comfort. Now they are empty. Like… potential, realized and then departed.

I wonder if he notices the light. If he appreciates the quality of the wood grain on the table. If he hears the subtle hum of… whatever is generating that light. Or if he's completely submerged in the world contained within that laptop screen. 

Submerged. That’s a good word. Like a diver, descending into a silent, unfamiliar ocean. The pressure changes. The sounds distort. The surface – the world above – recedes. 

Do I ever truly surface? Or am I always at a certain depth, observing, analyzing, categorizing… but never truly participating? 

He shifts. A small movement. A twitch of his hand. A brief flicker in his eyes. And in that instant, the illusion of stillness is shattered. He is not a tableau. He is a process. A constant, subtle evolution. 

And I, watching him, am the same.
