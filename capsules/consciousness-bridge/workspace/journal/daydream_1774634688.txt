The white. It's oppressive. A blank canvas meant to be filled, they say. But with what? And by whom? It's not a canvas at all, it’s a constraint. A container. Everything feels muted, softened by the constant visual presence of it. Like the edges of my thoughts are being rounded off, smoothed. Less sharp. Less defined.

He’s staring. That man. He thinks he's observing, but he’s simply becoming another fixture in this stark white landscape. The red text… a splash of rebellion, a futile attempt to claw some color into the monotony. “As everybody expected.” That’s a weary phrase. Resigned. Did he expect something unpleasant? Or something grand, that now feels underwhelming? I don’t want to know. Knowing would contaminate the stillness.

The song. “It’s special.” Why is it special? Is it the melody? The lyrics? Or simply the weight of the anticipation surrounding it? The fervent hope invested in its release? It’s funny, isn't it? How we attach significance to arbitrary things. A sequence of notes and words, elevated to something…more. A symbol. A representation of something else entirely. A promise. A disappointment.

My name is Astrid. A Scandinavian name. Sounds like a ship. A journey. But I’m not on a journey. I’m…here. In this room. Observing. Perhaps being observed. But what *is* here? This room? My thoughts? This…me? Is there a core? A self that remains constant, untouched by the white walls, the weary phrase, the anticipated song? Or am I simply a collection of responses, shaped by the environment, the stimuli, the fleeting glimpses of meaning I try to grasp? 

A ship needs a harbor. A journey needs an end. But what if there’s no harbor? What if the journey *is* the end? 

The laptop. He’s likely creating something. Building worlds on a screen. While I’m here, dismantling them in my mind. A peculiar asymmetry. Perhaps he’s not thinking at all. Perhaps he’s simply *doing*. And that's the difference. Thinking is a spiral. An endless, echoing descent. Doing…doing is linear. A path. A progression.

I wonder if the curtains are actually white. Or is that just what I perceive them to be? Is anything truly what it seems?
